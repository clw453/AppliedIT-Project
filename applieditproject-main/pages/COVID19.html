COVID-19 Response

The State of Colorado released new guidelines for Outdoor Recreation can be found here
If you have any questions concerning the following information please call us at 1-970-875-4494.
Colorado River Guides is currently working diligently to maintain and implement all local, state, and federal social distancing guidelines. Our primary concern has always been the safety of our guests and staff. To view a current copy of our Social Distancing Protocol required by Eagle County Click Here. For a more detailed description of our COVID-19 response please take a look below.

What Can You Expect From Us?

By limiting the sizes of our trips, we can ensure we are adhering to social distancing protocols enacted by local, state, and federal agencies. We are also offering completely private trips for groups that would prefer a totally private experience. If you are interesting in a private custom trip please give us a call at 1-970-875-4494.
Per local and state public health policy, we will continue to decrease vehicle capacity until instructed otherwise. Limiting vehicle capacity is just another way we ensure the integrity of enacted social distancing protocols. It's important to note; we have always met our guests right on the river. This means, you spend a very limited amount of time in our vehicles. However, the river still doesn't run in a circle, and we will need to transport guests either up river at the beginning of your river trip (and float you back to your car), or at the end of the rafting trip (back to your vehicle).
We will be using a disinfectant solution to clean all of our high-touch equipment and vehicles. If you have questions about whats in our disinfectant please email us at reservations@raftcolorado.com
Hand Sanitizer will be available for guests and employees when entering vehicles. Remember there aren't a lot of services such as running water etc. on the river and we encourage our guests to bring whatever supplies they may need to feel comfortable, including masks.
All employees will follow Eagle County latest public health order which includes daily symptom checks. These symptom checks will be conducted before the start of the work day. All employees have been instructed to not come to work, are encouraged to isolate, and contact their health providers if they are symptomatic. All employees have been made aware that diagnostic COVID testing is available in their area.
Employees who need to work in close proximity to guests and other employees will wear a mask, as mandated by Eagle County's latest public health order, masks will be worn until such public health order expires. Or unless the covering actively interferes with a situation in which the covering presents a potential hazard.
2020 we have a COVID-19 specific liability waiver that guests needed to sign. The goal of this waiver is to protect both participant's and Colorado River Guides. Simply put, A COVID specific waiver was one of the best ways to help us address the new and unique changes our industry has experienced this rafting season. If you have any questions about this waiver email us at reservations@raftcolorado.com.

At this time we ask our guests

Please maintain patience and flexibility, this is a confusing time for everyone. We are all learning a whole new meaning to the term 'go with the flow'!
That's totally okay! We understand, please call us and let us know whats going on. Give us a call or shoot us an email and we can go over any questions or concerns you have. We have a 24 hour cancellation policy, if something happens and you need to change your plans let us know as soon as possible so we can so we can help you make other arrangements.
Maintain 6ft of social distance whenever possible. We're all here to have a great day on the river, as long as everyone is aware of their surroundings we shouldn't have a problem.
Please bring a mask. It's also a good idea to check the most recent state and county health orders pertaining to mask mandates. As this information can change.
If you, or anyone in your group thinks they may be experiencing symptoms please stay home. Please understand we are not medical professionals and cannot give you any medical advice other than; being sick on vacation is a bummer, and isn't fun for anyone. If you think you are experiencing COVID-19 symptoms which can range from subtle to severe, call your doctor. If you have a rafting trip booked call us and we will cancel your trip. We have a 24 hour cancellation policy and we understand things happen.

Colorado River

Splash into the Colorado River in a raft or inflatable kayak! The Colorado River offers something for everyone, as a fun, splashy, and beautiful beginner whitewater rafting experience close to both Steamboat Springs and Vail. The Colorado river is perfect for the whole family. Come experience a scenic tour through the headwaters of the Colorado River today! We offer half day and full day depatures daily. Class I-III

Eagle River

The Eagle River is one of Colorado's last truly untamed & undammed rivers in the state. The Eagle River's snow-melt rapids make for an exciting once in a lifetime experience. Continuous whitewater & adrenaline pumping rapids makes the Eagle River an adventure you shouldn't miss. Half day and full day departures daily dependent on flows (May-June/July). Class I-IV


Book Now
