Going Rafting?
Here's What You'll Need!

Remember when you leave Steamboat Springs or Vail, it will be a bit cooler than where we meet you on the river. So remember to dress in layers. Cotton is not your friend when it comes to rafting in the Rocky Mountains.
Dress in synthetic layers, shorts, t-shirt, bathing suits, hats, sunscreen, bottles of water, and light snacks, are all fantastic! Remember to bring shoes you don’t mind getting wet. Flip-flops aren’t ideal, you want shoes that securely stay on your feet.
Bring a camera! There is some dry storage on the boats, but we still recommend the waterproof disposable cameras, some people like to use their phones, we don’t recommend that, over the years we’ve seen more than a few phones accidentally dropped into the river.

Eagle River

The Eagle River is one of Colorado's last truly untamed & undammed rivers in the state. The Eagle River's snow-melt rapids make for an exciting once in a lifetime experience. Continuous whitewater & adrenaline pumping rapids makes the Eagle River an adventure you shouldn't miss. Half day and full day departures daily dependent on flows (May-June/July). Class I-IV
